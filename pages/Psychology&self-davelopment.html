Psychology



( Thinking, Fast and Slow )

Allow me to just gush : This book is damn awesome, read it! Seriously though,
for behavioral research, there are few books that touch the scope and breadth that Dan Kahneman dives into with this masterpiece
Mr. Kahneman holds a Nobel Prize in economics as well, and this aspect shines through in the books many examples.

Evaluation : 9/10


(The Art of Choosing)

This is the quintessential read on how human beings make choices and what external influences affect those choices.
Needless to say, I was fascinated by the idea that choice can actually overwhelm, causing people to delay choosing rather than benefit from the extra options offered.
It is a fantastic read and very enjoyable all the way through, I happen to consider Sheena a great writer as well as a great researcher.

Evaluation : 8/10


(Made to Stick: Why Some Ideas Survive and Others Die )

Definitely one of my favorite marketing books ever written , but it is not something that can only be enjoyed by marketers.
This quote from Mark Twain is included in the books description: A lie can get halfway around
the world before the truth can even get its boots on.
The authors offer an explanation as to why these ideas can stay with us for so long.
this book aims to address the 6 ways certain ideas just stay with us while others slip away.

Evaluation : 8/10



self-davelopment



(How to Win Friends & Influence People )

This is the first self-improvement book. Written in 1937, by Carnegie can truly be called a classic.
It shows what we all intuitively know: it does not matter what your line of work is or what you want to achieve- if you are doing business of any kind, you need to make it about the other person. Being nice helps, a lot. this book does not distinguish between genuine interest and faking
it to get what you want, it still contains a treasure chest full of timeless wisdom.
this book still draws millions of readers to this day.

Evaluation : 9/10


(Think and Grow Rich: The Original 1937 Unedited Edition )

This book is all about material gain, and while it certainly covers that area, it is about much more than that.
Perhaps the first explicit mention of positive thinking, on how to care not just about the cash in your pocket,
but also the thoughts in your head, this book has been able to withstand the destruction of time.
It covers all the basics from planning, decision making and persistence .

Evaluation : 8/10


(The 48 Laws of Power)

How people use power for their own benefit and what to do to protect yourself from certain abuses of power.
Besides the fact that all stories in this book gravitate around power,
it contains many life lessons, amazing historical anecdotes and, if read in a certain light, the ability to use power for good.

Evaluation : 8/10
